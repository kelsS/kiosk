Pack Square

PREWAR

Once home to the obelisk erected in honor of Zebulon Vance, Pack Square was the hub of downtown Asheville at its best. Award-winning eateries lined the surrounding corners, and within a quarter mile the county courthouse, jail and city hall sat. Here we see Vance monument standing at the northern tip of downtown. Before the nuclear destruction, bronze statues of pigs and turkeys (at one time the main food sources of Earth from the 20th century until 2017) formed a line near the civil war memorial.

POSTWAR

After high society members fled Earth under rumors of imminent nuclear warfare, many Ashevillians surrounded Vance monument to protest the use of nuclear weapons in the war against North Korean terror attacks on China (two former great powers in Earth's history). Weeks later a bomb was dropped just outside of Asheville, but there was little time for civilians to flee the city before more bombs were dropped by planes flying over head, slaying hundreds of Asheville's residents and destroying most of the city itself.

This is where our ship will land to begin our tour. Our main kiosk is located here at Vance monument. If by chance you get separated from your tour group, please make your way back to our primary kiosk, which has a glowing blue light fixed to the top.
