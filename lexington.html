Lexington Avenue

PREWAR

This area of town came alive at night, before the riots began. Packed bars and thriving boutiques lined the street. Asheville's main economy was tourism (and so it is again). Tourists flocked to the local restaurants and shops here by the dozen.

POSTWAR

Lex 18

Many buildings on Lexington Avenue had been ransacked and pillaged by anyone lucky enough to have survived the initial attacks. There is some evidence which shows that at least a few people in the town survived. Unfortunately, it seems all of the survivors have now perished. Most likely from radiation poisoning, food contamination and scarcity, skirmishing, and animal attacks.
