Church Street

PREWAR

As a capitalist society, the United States was also strongly religious. On this street many 20th century antiquated facilities of adulation (formerly called "churches") hence the name, were home to the largest congregations. Gothic Revival and Romanesque styles of architecture (defunct architectural forms) as well as gorgeous arches and lovely gardens were once found on the "church" grounds.

POSTWAR

Out of range of the blast zones most of the churches remain intact. It is believed that this road became a shelter for the few original survivors. 200 year old artifacts were found in the Central United Methodist Church. Some preserved human tools have been excavated from the basement, suggesting that people lived there for some time.
